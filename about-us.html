Subdivisions

RaptorX comprises of four subdivisions

  • Mechanical

  • Avionics

  • Control & Software

  • Business

The Mechanical Subdivision stands as the stalwart vanguard dedicated to the relentless progression of every conceivable physical aspect within the domain of UAV (Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) technology. With an unwavering commitment to excellence, the team orchestrates a symphony of initiatives focused on amplifying the UAV's physical capabilities. This comprehensive endeavor spans exhaustive research into novel methodologies, advanced material sciences, and cutting-edge manufacturing techniques, all tailored to optimize component performance and durability.

The Avionics Subdivision is the link between the mechanical and software subdivisions, responsible for designing, manufacturing and managing the power requirements and controlling the thrusters and actuator systems with the help of customized PCBs.

Designing and Manufacturing

The Avionics Department serves as the technological nucleus for VTOL drone operations. Focused on integrating sophisticated electronic systems, it oversees navigation, communication, and control functions. Its tasks span sensor installation, GPS calibration, communication transceivers, flight control systems, and data transmission mechanisms. Collaborating across disciplines, the department ensures seamless integration and interoperability. Constantly advancing, it explores the latest in avionics technology, enhancing drone capabilities with cutting-edge sensors, refined navigation algorithms, and advanced communication protocols. This relentless pursuit of innovation ensures that VTOL drones outfitted with their avionics stand at the forefront of technological prowess, delivering not only efficiency and reliability but also cutting-edge performance.

The Software Subdivision is responsible for making RaptorX Autonomous! The The Software Subdivision is instrumental in rendering RaptorX autonomous. Leveraging C, C++, and Python, this division meticulously crafts a software stack comprising drivers, localisation, controller, navigator, simulator, state machine, mission planner, vision, and acoustics from the ground up. These modules are developed utilizing libraries like Robot Operating System (ROS) for inter-process communication and OpenCV for image processing, enabling the vehicle to autonomously make decisions. The software's architecture remains highly object-oriented and largely agnostic to specific vehicle models.Simulation in Xplane validates the software's functionality before real-life testing on the vehicle in the air, aligning with the subdivision's core objective of creating error-free, adaptable software to maximize the vehicle's operational potential.

Controller and Navigator

This system oversees the autonomous control of the vehicle's movement. The Controller manages the vehicle's attainment of specific positionsby utilizing feedback from localization.Efforts are ongoing to advance with more sophisticated Optimal Control Laws and refine the Vehicle’s Dynamics Models. The Navigator module facilitates the vehicle's navigation, employing Artificial Potential Fields Based Obstacle Avoidance and Trajectory Following Algorithms. It is tasked with dictating the necessary positions and velocities to the Controller. This component empowers the vehicle to adeptly navigate intricate underwater terrains, ensuring efficient movement and path traversal.
